## Overview

The Orang Dalam (Malay: 'people within' or 'hidden people') is a cryptid reported from the dense tropical rainforests of Johor, Malaysia's southernmost state, and occasionally from across the causeway in Singapore. Also known as Orang Mawas (or simply Mawas), the creature is described as a large, bipedal, black-furred humanoid standing approximately 2.4–3 meters (roughly 8–10 feet) tall. It is said to feed on fish and raid fruit orchards. Indigenous Orang Asli communities refer to a related entity as 'hantu jarang gigi' ('snaggle-toothed ghost'). Documented claims of Mawas sightings date back to 1871 according to the Wikipedia article on Orang Mawas. A wave of sightings in 2005–2006 attracted significant media attention, particularly in Johor, and prompted official investigation. The Johor wildlife department conducted searches but found no conclusive evidence. Scientific opinion generally identifies the creature as a misidentified sun bear (Helarctos malayanus), a species common in Johor's forests that can stand bipedally and whose claws produce large marks. No specimen, photograph, or unambiguous physical evidence has been confirmed. The creature is comparable to other Southeast Asian hominid cryptids including the orang-pendek of Sumatra and various Malay forest spirit traditions.

## Theories & Explanations

### Scientific

**Surviving or undescribed hominid** _(Some cryptozoological researchers)_

Some researchers propose the Orang Dalam represents a surviving population of Gigantopithecus or another undescribed large primate in the Malaysian rainforest.

### Skeptical

**Misidentified sun bear** _(Johor wildlife department, Malaysian wildlife biologists)_

The Malaysian sun bear (Helarctos malayanus), a species common in Johor's forests, can stand bipedally and move in ways that could be misinterpreted as a large humanoid. Sun bears are the standard scientific explanation for Orang Dalam reports.
